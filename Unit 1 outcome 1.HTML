The Purpose Of IT in Modern Business

In this modern world having a good IT infrastructure is essential for and business of any size. This includes having devices that are suitable and staff who know how to use them, even if your business only has one computer.

IT can take a repetitive and or time consuming task historically done by a human and do it over and over for accurate and consistent results, without tiring.

One way IT can save time for a business is by using a spreadsheet software to monitor cash flow and automatically work out VAT increases on products or services provided. You would need to set up a spreadsheet with the correct formulas in, but once this is done it can be used over and over again, thus saving time, not having to do it by hand.

The majority of people have access to the internet these days and are able to access it anywhere thanks to smartphones and tablets. So if your business does not have a website then you are severely limiting the potential customers you can reach.

IT can also allow customers to access your products and services without any human interaction, such as ordering products online. A customer can go to your website order something, have it delivered next day and never have any communication with someone who works for your company. This can save money by employing less staff and time because it could mean the staff you do have can work on other things.

The main things to consider are IT can save time, money and increase your exposure to potential customers from all around the world.

Describe Methods, Skills And Resources Needed To Complete Digital Successfully

There are several types of project management methods available. One is the waterfall methodology. This is a sequential idea that is heavily based on the requirement outcomes. You must have an idea of the what is expected before you can start as the scope cannot be changed once you have started. It is divided into stages which can be called; requirement, analysis, design, implement, testing, deployment and maintenance, each stage must be complete before you can move on to the next. This is easy to use but has a higher risk of failure due to errors often requiring the project to be started over.

You could have an agile method, this allow more flexibility and has a lower risk of failure, but is required to have more collection between the stakeholders which can delay things. An agile method is the opposite of a waterfall method.

You can also have a hybrid method, which combines the best features of both the waterfall style and the agile style. This gives you increased flexibility but requires a compromise as you are trying to coordinate to opposite ends of a spectrum.

Describe The Risks That Might Impact Digital Projects

There are many risks that could impact upon digital projects, risks are a reality and can cause projects to go over time and budget.

One example of something that could go wrong; say you are in the middle of 12 month project to build a new website. 9 months in Apple could release a new iOS version meaning that all the work done over the last 9 months wouldn't work properly on this new iOS.

Another risk could be cyber attack, It is likely that you will have data stored on your customers which you have a legal obligation to protect. Hackers could attempt to access this data and sell it on. So a robust security system is needed. This could also help prevent virus's.

One last example of something that could go wrong is a key project members leaves the project mid way through, this would leave a potential gap in knowledge and skills. So it is important to have a system in place where staff can keep trace of what they have done so new staff can pick up where the older staff left off.

Legal Guidelines and Constraints

One legal this to be concerned about when running digital projects is data protection. Now know as GDPR, companies that hold personal data on the customers such as names, addresses, dates of birth and credit card numbers, and have a duty to keep this data safe and only access when needed. If data is lost, stolen or used when it is not needed, this can result in fines for business. So having effective data security is essential.

Another legal thing to consider is health and safety at work. While sitting down at a computer might not seem dangerous, it can cause injury. For example typing all day may create strains in peoples wrists, so as an employer you are obliged to provide your staff with ergonomic mice and keyboards. Staff should also be allowed regular breaks and looking at a computer screen for long periods of time can strain peoples eyes.

Copyright and other constraints on websites

Copyright is a key concern when creating a website, as you need permission to use certain images found online. You are able to buy stock images or you can get written consent from the image owner to use their image on your site, if you do this you will probably be required to give credit to the image owner. Another constraint affecting websites is that of many people using smartphones to view content. While PC screens are long and not tall, most smartphones are tall and not long. So a website needs to be able to be displayed in both versions. This is often called desktop view and mobile view.

Overcoming issues for disabled users

One issue facing disabled users could be that of poor eyesight. This means that a website with small text would be difficult to read. This can be overcome by having websites with an option to display in large font, or switching between colour and black and white. Another factor could be users with limited use in their hands. This means they might not be able to click on lots of links or scroll easily. So having a website with minimal scrolling and pages could help these users. Users with hearing problems will not be effect by my website as there are no music or sounds.
